Echo Lock — feasibility mock

Phone at normal listening position, volume at session level. Each run plays a 1.4s pattern through this device's speaker, listens through its mic, and measures how long the sound took to come back. Three runs agreeing within ~20ms = the physics works on this hardware.

What this proves: (1) iOS gives us raw mic audio while playing — echo cancellation, if it can't be disabled, will erase the very signal we measure; (2) envelope cross-correlation finds a single sharp peak in a real room. The real build correlates against the playing track instead of a test pattern, measures the room and this device separately, and writes the difference straight into sync trim.
