History & Background

The Trials of Kirtas

A kingdom's rise, a dynasty's fracture, and twenty years of war that left a realm in ashes.

The Kingdom of Kirtas

Kirtas, the jewel of the Western Kingdoms. Occupying a fertile valley between the Kharak Mountains to the South-East and the Long Hills to its North and West, for centuries this human kingdom was the dominant force in the region. To the North-East lay the great Elven forest Ilora, whose finely crafted goods flowed into Kirtas, traded in exchange for the kingdom's renowned farmed produce, livestock, fine wines and cheeses.

The Elven goods and other valuables would travel down the Kir River to the coast of the Andan Sea and the great city of Tiersgard, and from there to parts far and wide, bringing in vast wealth.

Friendship with the Elves brought great opportunities to the kingdom, whose artisans learned to create fine jewelry and crafts from their elder allies. So too did the Wizard's colleges benefit, acquiring great learning, coveted scrolls and ancient books through trade with lands both distant and near. This arcane knowledge and craft were put to use by the kingdom to project its power. The Kirtian Military was a powerful force which brought the surrounding realms into vassalage while pushing Orcs, Goblins and other monstrosities out of the land. Thus the dominance of the Ranec Dynasty, kings of Kirtas, was maintained for centuries while the land grew ever more prosperous.

The Succession Crisis

The previous king, Aluin Ranec III, had assumed the throne as a young man and ruled vigorously for decades. Unable to produce a male heir, his younger brother Tyrus was named his successor. However, shortly before the king's death of pox, one of his concubines unexpectedly became pregnant.

Just two months after Tyrus's ascension, a prince with a direct claim to the throne was born to the concubine. Crisis gripped the land as the king's council and his most senior general, Darius, supported the young prince. Tyrus refused to abdicate and with his supporters marched on Yevennia where the prince and his supporters were based. War descended on Kirtas.

The young prince has not been seen for years. Many whisper that he died at the hands of an assassin — that Darius and the council seek to rule in his name and so hide his death.

✦
Twenty Years of War

The ensuing decades of conflict have ravaged the once rich land and its populace. With loyalties divided, the mighty armies of Kirtas were split apart and bled dry. The kingdom's coffers were bankrupted to hire armies of mercenaries. These hardened bands plundered and pillaged the land when it suited them, bringing ever more misery and desperation to the citizens.

With no men to fill them, the border watches lapsed and foul creatures were drawn to the bloodshed and misery like flies to a feast. Armies of vicious Hobgoblins and Orcs poured in from the mountains to raid what wealth and plunder they could find. Increasingly desperate to put a decisive end to the conflict, wizards from each side turned to forbidden knowledge and began raising the dead from the battlefield with foul necromancy. Many of these abominations escaped the control of their masters and now plague large swaths of the kingdom.

Seeing the devastation and folly wrought on their border, the Elves of Ilora have withdrawn to their woodland realm and keep a vigilant watch there, severing contact with the outside world. Those that stray near seldom return.

The World Today

All this Kirtas was. Today, the kingdom is a husk of its former self. Within the fractured kingdom some cities and towns have managed to escape the worst of the devastation by organizing militias, stockpiling weapons and food. Other areas have been given over fully to Orcs, goblins and other foulness.

Only Tiersgard, the coastal trade city, retains a semblance of its former glory — its merchants still making tidy profits by importing the tools of war. The city remains ostensibly neutral, but agents from each side operate within its walls, seeking information and advantage.

Today, the remnants of each side still skirmish and make attempts to hold their territory against encroachment, but grand offensive campaigns are increasingly rare. Tyrus and his forces still hold the capital and dream of unifying the kingdom under his banner. Darius and Lord Reykoldt are encamped somewhere in the Long Hills with what forces they still control.

And to the south, on the other side of the Kharak range, the kingdom of Numior — once vassals of Kirtas — are hiring mercenaries of their own, perhaps seeing the opportunity to claim land and treasure from those who ruled them for so long.
